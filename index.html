About Ramak Jaberizadeh

Registered Psychotherapist (Qualifying) dedicated to providing compassionate, evidence-based therapy tailored to your unique needs and goals.

My Approach

I work with youth and adults navigating anxiety, depression, grief, relationship challenges, life transitions, identity exploration, cultural dynamics, and the effects of trauma, displacement, and marginalization. You are the expert of your life; my role is to support you in reconnecting with your inner wisdom and strengths.

With a background in communications and counselling psychology, I've come to deeply appreciate how language shapes our sense of self and helps us make meaning of our experiences. In our work together, we'll explore new perspectives, reflect on helpful and limiting beliefs, and reclaim your narrative in a way that feels authentic and empowering.

Meaningful shifts tend to happen when we feel safe enough to be honest. That's what I strive to offer — a relationship where you can show up as you are, speak openly, and trust you'll be met with respect, care, and understanding.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about therapy and what you can expect from our work together.

How do I know if therapy is right for me?

Therapy can benefit anyone who wants support navigating life's challenges — whether you're dealing with a specific issue or simply want to understand yourself better. If you're feeling stuck, overwhelmed, or just curious about your patterns, therapy can help.

What can I expect in my first session?

Your first session is a chance for us to get to know each other. We'll talk about what brings you to therapy, your goals, and your history. It's also an opportunity for you to ask questions and see if we're a good fit.

How long does therapy typically take?

The length of therapy varies depending on your goals and needs. Some people benefit from a few sessions, while others prefer ongoing support over months or years. We'll regularly review your progress and adjust our approach accordingly.

Is therapy confidential?

Yes. Everything you share in therapy is strictly confidential. There are limited exceptions required by law — such as if there is risk of harm to yourself or others — which I'll explain fully at the start of our work together.

Do you accept insurance?

Many extended health benefit plans cover services provided by a Registered Psychotherapist. I recommend checking with your insurer to confirm your coverage. I can provide receipts for reimbursement.

What's the difference between therapy and counseling?

While the terms are often used interchangeably, psychotherapy typically involves deeper, longer-term work exploring patterns, emotions, and the root causes of distress. Counselling often focuses on specific issues and shorter-term solutions.

Can I do therapy online?

Yes. I offer both in-person and secure online sessions. Online therapy is just as effective as in-person for most concerns and offers the flexibility of attending from wherever you feel comfortable.

What if I don't feel comfortable with you as my therapist?

The therapeutic relationship is the most important factor in effective therapy. If at any point you don't feel our approach is working for you, I encourage you to say so. I'll do my best to adjust or help you find someone who's a better fit.
